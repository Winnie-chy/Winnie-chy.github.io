The Study of Agriculture

Due to the epidemic, we put off our farming practice until before this summer vacation. Before the departure, I was a little nervous because the experience of military training made me worry about whether the study of agriculture should also stipulate the time for eating and bathing and whether there is also a drillmaster strict discipline.

After arriving at the destination, I found that this is a remote place that surrounded by a series of mountains instead of tall buildings. Although there is no hustle and bustle of the city and the air is fresher, we still have to work outdoors all day in the sun. In the following three days, we learned how to plant sweet potatoes, weave straw sandals, grind soybean milk and so on. We also had a 3km hike in one evening and enjoyed a firework display in another evening. During this hard time, I learned some things such as knowing that a sweet potato plant must be planted diagonally into the soil so that several potatoes can be produced. Otherwise, only one potato can be harvested if the plant is inserted straight into the soil. There I saw the horses and cattle in the fields that I have never seen in my daily life as well. What impressed me most was that my classmates and I made dumplings for lunch together. Although some of us didn’t know how to make dumplings and made them in weird shape, this activity truly promoted the fraternity and unity between us. Generally, I felt very hot and tired every day, but I experienced the precious dormitory life. Living with my classmates and friends for two nights was fantastic that we could chatted with each other even after the lights out.

Later, we have an activity to learn sign language. Through the teacher’s introduction, I know that here is also a special school for children with cerebral palsy and mental retardation or deaf and dumb. They can’t keep up with the fast pace of normal schools in cities, and some children are afraid of the crowded and noisy environment, so they set up a special school in this remote mountain area. After one hour of learning, I found that learning sign language is just like learning
another new language, which is difficult to learn well and communicate with people in a short time because lots of similar words have totally different body expressions. These vulnerable groups arouse my sympathy and after I went home, I searched for some information online and get some data about these kinds of special schools. In 2019, there were 2,192 special education schools nationwide and the number of special education students in China reached 794,600 which was 128,700 more than in 2018, or 19.3 percent more than in 2018. I am glad to see that the government is paying more and more attention to caring for the disabled instead of giving up. The investment in children’s education is also increasing year by year. The society has also provided more positions for the disabled, so that everyone can be treated equally and have the opportunity to make contributions to the country and society.

In addition, the three-day life on the farm work training site helped me understand the Chinese saying “Every grain comes from hard working” and I hope every disabled child there can grow up happily.

Reunderstanding Health

With the development of the times, people’s material life has become more abundant, and the quality of life has risen rapidly all over the world. Therefore, more and more people hope to have a longer lifetime, so people generally concentrate more on the topic about health.

Due to the Career Experience Day organized by the school after CIE exam, I had the opportunity to visit and experience Cooper Aerobics Company. It was the first time for me to have this experience and enter the health field that I have never known. The car took us into the mountains, where our destination was hidden in: Cooper’s Aerobics. When we walk through the door, the staff did not rush to let us start the visit, first she let us have some understanding of the real “health”. Cooper Aerobics has eight rules for maintaining health which are:
1.Take a regular, comprehensive physical exam
2.Exercise 5 days a week
3.Maintain a healthy weight
4.Make healthy food choices most of the time
5.Take the right supplements for you
6.Control alcohol
7.Don’t smoke/ Use tobacco
8.Manage your stress

I thought the Career Experience Day was always just an all-day-long lecture, but to my surprise, we actually started exercising in the morning. Fifteen of us divided into two groups, completed tests of flexibility (sit and reach), strength (push-ups) and endurance (one-mile race walk) to see how old we were in terms of physical fitness. I have no exercise for a long time, so I deeply felt the necessity of regular exercise. Lunch was not quite what I had expected, either. Eating less is not the right way to stay healthy and every meal should be full. The ratio of meat, vegetables and staple food must be 1:1:2. The dishes there were low calorie meals with less oil and salt than normal restaurant, which are delicious and healthy. In the afternoon, we still carried on the exercise, this time is the mental and physical coexist small games which were different from the morning. Through the two games, the staff there led us to understand the importance of teamwork. Only with a unified goal, a clear division of labor and strict observance of the rules can the task be completed quickly and well. Our team narrowly beat the odds and won a mug for each of us.

When I got home, I searched the Internet for more information about Cooper Aerobics Company. Its founder, Dr. Kenneth Cooper, is the personal doctor of former President George W. Bush, the world’s “father of aerobics”, a pioneer in the field of sports and public health around the world, and a master of “curing disease” in Western medicine. In the 1960s, Dr. Cooper discovered the relationship between aerobic exercise and health while doing physical testing for American astronauts in NASA. He invented the word “Aerobics” and published the best-selling book Aerobics which swept the world in 1968, profoundly affecting the world. He changed people’s old ideas and health conditions and opened a revolution in fitness exercise that people all over the world love – aerobic exercise. Dr. Kenneth Cooper was therefore honored as the “Father of Aerobic Exercise” in the world. (From: http://www.cooperaerobics.cn )

In conclusion, I don’t think people should lose weight blindly and regard thinness as beauty. Healthy is the most important thing that everyone should pay attention to. Follow Cooper’s eight health rules and have regular check-ups, I’m sure all of us will become healthier and healthier.

Thinking on Population Crisis

Last year, 2020, was a very important year in human history. In this year, people all over the world have experienced a lot and everyday good or bad news was spread everywhere. While every country is busy and chaotic, South Korea reported that its registered population for the whole of 2020 will be 51.83 million, down 21,000 from the previous year on January 3rd.

As we learned in Economic class, there are many standards to estimate the development of a country such as the death rate and the birth rate are important indexes and references. It was the first time in South Korean history that there have been more deaths than births. South Korea’s fertility rate (the number of children per woman of childbearing age) decreased rapidly from around 1.2 in 2016 to 0.92 in 2019, with less than one child per woman. The same thing happened in Taiwan that the number of births in Taiwan dropped to 165,200 in 2020, with 173,100 deaths. This is also the first time in Taiwan’s history that the population has experienced negative growth. In Asia, however, Japan’s population crisis has already occurred, and population aging is also the worst. Japan’s population reached an all-time high of 128 million in 2009. But since then, Japan has experienced 11 years of negative population growth, with the total population falling to 125 million by July 2020. In 2019, the proportion of population over 65 in Japan has reached 28.4%, ranking first in the world. The worst thing is that if South Korea is where Japan was ten years ago, China’s demographic situation will soon be like South Korea’s. There is no denying that a demographic crisis is spreading across East Asia.

There are two reasons that I came up with to explain this phenomenon. One is that women are becoming more educated, and the rate of women who choose to delay or even not marrying increase. The average age of first marriage for South Korean women has risen from 24.8 in 1990 to 30.4 today. The age at which women had their first child was also delayed to 31.6. As the level of education increases, women begin to take the initiative to ask for the right to work, to be paid more, to be in charge of themselves, and not to be regarded as a housewife who can only “take care of the husband and raise the children”. As a result, they chose not to marry or have children to pursue their own careers and freedom, directly challenging the traditional patriarchal society. Second, the cost of raising children is too high, resulting in too much economic pressure to have children. Highly developed urban economy with high price level and unreachable housing prices make family with middle income suffer from debts. In Japan, the cost of a child’s 15 years from kindergarten to high school is about 5.23 million yen. However, the average annual income of Japanese people is only about 4 million yuan. Also, in South Korea, the cost of raising a child is about 14 million won a year on average. The average South Korean worker earns 41 million won a year and spends a third of his income on raising children. Moreover, there is an opportunity cost to having children for women. During their pregnancy leave, their jobs are possibly be replaced by others, resulting in missing the chances for promotion and salary increase. Many companies are also reluctant to recruit women who are possible to have children because it’s a waste of company’s efficiency and performance if women have pregnancy leave with their paid in the future.

The possible solution is that government should raise their spending in order to increase the fertility rate. First of all, in order to eliminate the public’s stereotype of women and increase women’s rights, the government can advertise more to increase their awareness and call for equality. Also, government can offer subsidies to support middle class families who have more than one child such as provide more money welfare and baby products to improve their living standard. What’s more, to make women receive more equal treatment, the government can require companies to give women enough holidays and reserved positions to encourage them to have children without worrying about their careers.

It seems that the key point is that the birth rate of a country is decreasing, but in fact it reflects the proof of New Age Women for their economic independence and working ability and challenge to the inequality between men and women in the past. Hopefully, with the intervention of the government and the change in the minds of the people in the new era, every country will get an appropriate fertility rate.

A Midsummer Night's Dream

This National Day holiday, my mother and I went to the Poly Theatre to enjoy the play midsummer night’s dream. To be honest, I didn’t know anything about drama before. It was also my first time to see a drama, so I lacked the knowledge about drama.

All stage plays are divided into several categories, as shown in the picture below. Among them, I focus on the drama. Drama mainly based on dialogue. It is a comprehensive art with script creation, director, performance, stage design, lighting and commentary indispensable. It came to China in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Different from traditional stage plays and operas, the main way to performance of drama is the unaccompanied dialogues or monologues of the actors on the stage, accompanied by a small amount of music and singing. The introduction of European drama into China led to the rise of Modern Chinese drama. They reflect social life through characters, so dialogues are personalized, natural, refined, vivid, beautiful, expressive, easy to understand and can be accepted by the public.

After searching the Internet, these are quite different from the drama I originally thought. Since Cats and Phantom of the Opera are so famous, I have seen some clips in my music class at school and thought that all dramas are stories composed of a lot of music and dance like them. So, I think the midsummer night’s dream is also the kind of romantic comedy story performed by dancing in a very beautiful background. It wasn’t until I realized that I was watching an adapted play that I thought it was very “Chinese style”. The adapted one not only changes the names of the characters, but also changes lots of the plots. Exaggerations are used to increase the numbers of punchlines, which makes people burn into a laugher and feel like watching a comedy sketch you see at the Spring Festival gala.

At the same time, I also quite admire the skills of these actors. I think it is very hard to rehearse a perfect drama. Acting drama totally different from shooting TV series or movies that if actors are not satisfied, the whole movie clips can be shot again. Drama is instantaneous and performed in front of all the audience. Therefore, every detail of the performance such as every line and actor’s expression and emotions should be the most perfect. Therefore, actors need to have a strong ability to improvise. Even if they encounter some unexpected situations during the performance, they should try their best to solve them without letting the audience see the flaws and then continue to complete the performance.

Due to the adaptation of the play place too much, to be honest I am a little bit disappointed to this drama. To my view, I prefer to see the original a midsummer night’s dream, or in the other words, through the information of the stage, I prefer to see the opera than the drama because they have beautiful dancing and singing, bring a person the edification and enjoy the beauty of art.

About Saving Food

Chinese people are famous for their hospitality all over the world. It has almost become a Chinese tradition that the host will take friends and relatives to a nearby restaurant for big meals when guests come to visit the host.

In order to satisfy the guests as well as Chinese people don’t want others to think they are poor, they usually order more than enough food and this both a waste of money and food. Food waste has become a serious problem especially during the peak period of holidays. The government had to encourage the rule “N-1” which means every time order one less dishes than the number of people. However, according to the research of some economists, there are simpler ways than the “N-1” rule.

The first method is about the psychological “ Dolboef illusion”, in which background affects a lot when people make judgements about the shape of an object. As shown in the picture below, the area of black circle in the middle on the right looks larger than that on the left but in fact, the black circle is the same. Economists associate this phenomenon with food waste so in the same way, people will feel that the same amount of food is larger on a small plate than on a large one and will not order as much food without realizing. In 2012, two economists called Kallbekken and Salen worked with hotels on a study of food waste and plate size. In 2013 Pelle Guldborg Hansen, another economist, also became interested in the link between plate size and food savings and concluded that a 3cm reduction in plate diameter resulted in a 26% reduction in food waste. Therefore, reducing the diameter of the plate can really effectively reduce food waste.

Another useful way also uses psychology to guide people’s decisions through small changes which called nudge theory from behavioral economist Richard Taylor (2017 Nobel Prize in Economics). He mentioned four rules which are easy, attractive, social, timely and collectively called “EAST”. For two of them, easy and social, have simple examples. Firstly, it’s all known that push a heavy object against the table forced and because of friction, the object will soon stop moving. Human motivation also reduced when face a lot of troubles. As a result, reducing the size of plate also helps save food because people have to run more times to get the same amount of food. Then for social, humans are social animals, and the stress of not fitting in can lead people to change their behavior. For instance, SAN Marcos, California has introduced a new electricity bill to encourage local households to use less electricity. People will be able to see not only their own bills in recent weeks, but also the average use of electricity by their neighbors. When people find that they use far more electricity than their neighbors, they gradually reduce their use of electricity. The same goes for saving food.

A rational person will never order too much and waste food. But real people are not entirely rational. As a result, some helps from economists will hopefully make us live better.

The Game Theory

In economics, there is a word everyone is familiar with called opportunity cost which means the best alternative forgone. Also, when I knew the concept of game theory in an economic book, I realized that in some ways, game theory was very similar to opportunity cost.

When producers make an economic decision, it certainly contain gains and losses. That’s why they have to make the optimal decision after careful consideration and estimation of advantages and disadvantages. Unlike opportunity cost, game theory is not about making a decision, but about two people using and changing their strategies in order to win in an equal game. Game theory considers the predictive behavior and actual behavior of individuals in the game and studies their optimization strategies.

In game theory, the most famous and easy-understood example is the ‘Prisoner’s Dilemma’ model given by Tucker. The model tells us the story of a policeman and a thief in a special way. Suppose two thieves conspire to commit a crime and are caught by the police.

The police put the two men in separate rooms for questioning. The policy was: If both suspects confessed and the evidence was conclusive, both were convicted, and each had to spend eight years in prison. If only one of the suspects confesses and the other denies the confession, the person who denied will be given an additional two years in prison, and the offender’s sentence will be reduced by eight years and will be released immediately. If both men deny it, the police can’t convict them on other charges for lack of evidence but can send each man to prison for one year. The following table shows the payment matrix for this game.

A\B confess deny
confess 8\8 0\10
deny 10\0 1\1

From the table, since neither person knows what the other will choose, confession is always optimal because it makes sure you’re not at a disadvantage between two people. But if they all choose to “deny”, they will each be sentenced to only one year in prison. As a result, (denial, denial) is Pareto optimal, because any other action choice combination that deviates from this action choice combination will make a person in a worse situation. However, “confession” is the dominant strategy of any criminal suspect, and (confession, confession) is a dominant strategic equilibrium, Nash equilibrium. It is not hard to see that there is a conflict between Nash equilibrium and Pareto here.

Of course, this example is just for a better understanding of game theory, and it doesn’t work in psychologically or socially ways. I don’t understand this complicated concept at first, but after seeing this example, I think game theory is two people making decisions without letting each other know. And that’s why there is more uncertainty than the opportunity cost.

Velvet Flowers Memory One

I never thought that one day I would have contact with the intangible cultural heritage of Jiangsu Province before. Since my childhood, I only knew that one of my relatives was a great craftsman, and I didn’t know that he was the inheritor of velvet flower until later this craft was gradually becoming world-famous.

Velvet flowers mean glory in ancient China which has a quite positive wishes and is one of the representatives of the wealth in Chinese culture. They are made by silk manually after more than ten processes so that a piece of work usually takes more than two weeks.

I was just amazed by the deliciated works of animals or flowers I have seen before at their lifelike but never thought about the process of making these works. It wasn’t until I had the opportunity to learn each step of the process myself that I realized how difficult the technique was. Just for the first two steps, firstly was to straighten out the silk. I was quite confident that I can deal with it since I saw other people can do this quickly and nicely just by combing using fingers. But until I did it myself, no matter what I did, even with a brush, I could not make the silk smooth and neat enough. These knotted silks would surely affect the next few steps. Then I had to fold the thin copper wire in half and twist the top together so that the copper wire clamped the silk in the middle. Although the instructions were simple and clear, it took me a lot of effort just to rub up the two ends of the copper wire because I sometimes rubbed out the copper oxide and led to the loss of friction which made the copper wire become useless.

On the one hand, the non-genetic inheritor is faced with the survival problem. On the other hand, due to the great pressure of social employment and survival, even if some college students are interested in making velvet, they will eventually give up this ideal for some reason which lead to the lack of successors for this unique technique. Now the inheritances try their best to combine the flower with the trend. Through TV dramas and advertisements, they hope that people all over the world can realize the beauty of the flower and prevent the loss of this precious skill. He also set up a company and recruited staff to increase the output and efficiency of his works in order to customize products and offer better services.

There are many other rare and little-known skills in Chinese culture besides the velvet flowers such as making clay figurines and weaving Chinese knots. I hope that these ancient skills will be valued by the young generation instead of being forgotten with the development of the times.

The First IELTs Test

This week, our family went to Yangzhou in order to take my first IELTs test. Because of the spread of covid-19, many exam centers have been cancelled, so I have to take the exam in Yangzhou.

The first is the trip to Yangzhou. It was a really hard trip for all of us since we not only had to pay extra fee for the hotel and gas, but also went there twice which took for four hours each time. However, I have to admit that Yangzhou does have some impressive local snacks, and I took the opportunity to taste a lot of delicious food. What I liked the most is the baozi there because every kind of baozi there with different flavors is full of praise. As a result, we brought many baozi back to Nanjing. Moreover, the night before my speaking test, my parents and I decided to go shopping in Dongguan Street together after dinner. It was a place similar to Confucius Temple and Lao Mendong in Nanjing, where modern shops combined with ancient architectural styles to create a lively scene and a wide variety of goods for consumers to choose from.

As for IELTs, I was also full of nervousness and curiosity, because I have never experienced such an international formal exam before, and I feel extremely upset when I meet foreigners face to face. Before the test, I prepared all the test supplies in advance, such as the admission ticket and a bottle of water without cover. When I actually entered the examination room, I found that the rules were stricter than any exam I had ever taken. We were not only not allowed to wear anything like metal necklaces, earrings, but also our body had to be scanned. I wondered why I had to enter the exam room 30 minutes early at the beginning, but when I arrived, I realized that almost all the time had been spent verifying identities. We were all carefully compared with our ID cards, photographed and even our fingerprints were recorded. It’s the first time I experienced such a strict check. Luckily, I found that the examiners are kind enough that made me feel less stressed. Everything here is well-organized, the staff is also very responsible and instructions are very clear. Those really provide a lot of convenience for the first test candidates like me.

In addition, I hope I can pass the IELTS exam as soon as possible since I don’t want to carry this burden to the second year which already full of academic achievements.

The Biggest Failure I Experienced

To be honest, I haven’t experienced what I consider to be a significant failure, but there are things I regret.

It was the high school entrance exam, and we were going to have an English human-computer speaking test. However, I didn’t take the test seriously at all, since it sounded like most people could get full marks.

Before the exam, we had a booklet that covered all the topics that could be selected and all we needed to do was to recite the articles fluently. I looked through the booklet and found that the articles were very simple and short, so I thought it was quite an easy test and there was no need to take time to practice them. When my classmates all rushed to the teacher to recite the passage and even when someone started to recite the passage for a second time, I still refused to go to the teacher’s office. Finally, the teacher forced me to go to her office. I recited every day like everyone else, but I never took it to heart and forgot the articles just after I recited them to the teacher. In this way, on the day of the exam, however, I began to panic when I saw that others were confident. Because the passages were not memorized, I could only guess in the exam.

After the test results came out, in the test where most people got full marks, I actually had one point deducted. Even some people who normally didn’t do as well as me got better marks than I did. I was shocked and couldn’t believe this result and regretted that I had underestimated the exam at the beginning. We only sit the high school entrance examination once in our life and in such an important exam, every point counts. It has passed and I cannot retake it. I did not get full marks because of overconfidence and an inability to improvise.

From this experience, I learned the lesson that for study, no matter how small tests are, we shouldn’t be lazy and ignore them. Careful preparation is the only way to get the high marks that you expect. This applies to life in general and not just to studying. It is worth taking even small things seriously. One should never dismiss small things as you don’t know how much impact they might have.
