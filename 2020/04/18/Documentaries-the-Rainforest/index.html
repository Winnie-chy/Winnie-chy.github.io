Documentaries-the Rainforest

I recently took a course to learn English by watching BBC documentaries, and I gradually got to know this colorful world. I found that in all parts of the world, there are so many unspoiled amazing landscapes, which are home to rare animals and plants.

First, I came to rainforest. At any time here, countless species are fighting for space and food. Also, competition force animals to specialize in every aspect of their life.

What impressed me the most is a kind of hummingbird called swordbills. They are the only bird with a beak longer than their body. As a result, they don’t have to join the competition for sweet nectar with other kinds of hummingbirds. I was attracted by their unique appearance, and then I searched the Internet for more information. To my surprised, there are really two sides to everything. Although they don’t have to worry about running out of food, they can never comb their feathers with their beaks. So how they clean their feathers? They can only use their claws to clean up. It seems that animals are doing their best to survive and at the same time, they must give up something.

Not only are animals finding ways to survive, but plants are also “getting smarter”. For example, scientists have found a kind of luminous fungus. You can watch a wonder of the rainforest when night came. The reason is that the light attracts insects. As insects will cover in the fungus’s spores, insects carry these spores to other parts of the forest. It successfully got me interested in plants.

However, there are also moments that make me feel heavy. The monkey called indri is humans’ friends and brothers for a long time. But in recent years, one million hectares of the rainforest have been destroyed in Madagascar alone, and, with it, half the indri families that once lived there before. Local people believed that their song is a call to remind us that we, too, once depended on the jungle.

The Eden is a place of wonder and magic. I was attracted by the colorful birds, the strange-looking plants, the competition and courtship between animals in the mysterious rain forest. I believe that all the natural features and species are a gift to human. We must protect variety of species instead destroying it.
